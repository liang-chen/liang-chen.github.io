About me

I'm currently a Ph.D. candidate in Music Informatics at Indiana University Bloomington, advised by Prof. Christopher Raphael.
My research interest is to apply computational approaches and statistical models to address the problems related to the acquisition, analysis and processing of music data. Some specific projects include the scalable system for Optical Music Recognition (OMR), automatic music renotation from OMR, and expressive instrument synthesis. In essence, my research explores how to better combine technology such as AI, Computer Vision and HCI, and music to create useful or interesting applications.

I've created a thesis latex template to facilitate the people who may need to spend much time formatting their thesis due to the official requirements of our school.
